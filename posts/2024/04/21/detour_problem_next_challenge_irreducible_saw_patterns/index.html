Detour problem’s next challenge: irreducible saw patterns

2024/04/21

To facilitate our discussion, let's expand the existing definitions and make a few things formal. For $u,v \in V$, let's refer to a simple path in $G-\mathcal{B}_{s,t}+u+v$ (ie. completely outside $\mathcal{B}_{s,t}$ except the end points) from $u$ to $v$ as a hop from $u$ to $v$. We say it is forward if $dist(s,u) < dist(s,v)$, lateral if $dist(s,u) = dist(s,v)$, and backward if $dist(s,u) > dist(s,v)$. Call a consecutive sequence of backward hops a backward flight.

The central idea behind the flawed algorithm is that we can try to detect detours by detecting certain features. So far this idea works well when the feature is a forward hop, a lateral hop, or a "nice" backward hop or flight (using the "pebbling game" in Fortune et al.).
